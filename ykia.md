# Instructions for Generating a Finnish YKI-style Speaking Test

This document provides guidelines for creating a standardized speaking test set based on the Finnish National Certificate of Language Proficiency (YKI) framework.

## 1. Structure and Task Types

Every speaking test set must consist of four specific task types, each with its own timing and preparation requirements:

### A. Kertominen (Narrating)

- **Description:** The candidate speaks continuously on a given topic.
- **Timing:** 1 minute for preparation; 1.5 to 2 minutes for speaking [1, 2].
- **Content:** Provide a main heading and 4–5 bullet points to guide the candidate (e.g., "An important place": where is it, why is it important, what do you do there?) [2, 3].

### B. Keskustelu (Conversation)

- **Description:** A simulated dialogue where the candidate responds to pre-recorded turns.
- **Format:** Represent the interlocutor's turns with stars (\*\*\*) and provide candidate instructions in brackets (e.g., "Respond affirmatively") [4, 5].
- **Timing:** Response windows vary between 5 and 30 seconds per turn [4, 6].

### C. Tilanteet (Situations)

- **Description:** Short, spontaneous reactions to social scenarios.
- **Timing:** 20 seconds to read the prompt; 10 to 30 seconds for speaking [7, 8].
- **Content:** Define a clear scenario, such as reporting a broken appliance to a landlord or apologizing for being late [9-11].

### D. Mielipide (Opinion)

- **Description:** A reasoned argument regarding a specific statement or topic.
- **Timing:** 2 minutes for preparation; 2 minutes for speaking [12, 13].
- **Content:** Present a statement (e.g., "Living in Finland is too expensive") followed by abstract or societal questions to encourage deeper reasoning [12, 14, 15].

## 2. Core Themes to Include

The questions should cover a variety of life areas as found in the sources:

- **Personal Background:** Family, education, and language learning motivation [1, 16-18].
- **Housing:** Home descriptions, neighbor relations, and residential area quality [19-21].
- **Work and Education:** Work experience, job interviews, and professional dreams [22-24].
- **Daily Services:** Banking, library usage, and handling service complaints [25-27].
- **Health:** Describing symptoms to a doctor or making an emergency call [28-30].
- **Environment & Society:** Recycling, travel, voting, and societal issues like taxes [31-34].

## 3. Proficiency Level Distinctions

- **Level 3 (Concrete):** Focus on personal experience and concrete topics [35].
- **Level 4 (Abstract):** Focus on societal impacts and abstract reasoning (e.g., the role of the state in supporting culture) [35, 36].

## 4. Official YKI Speaking Topics (Aihealueet)

All speaking and writing tasks must be grounded in one of the nine official topic areas:

| # | Topic (Finnish) | Description |
|---|-----------------|-------------|
| 1 | **Minä ja taustani** | Personal background, family, languages, identity |
| 2 | **Koti ja asuminen** | Home, neighbourhood, housing, daily routines |
| 3 | **Kauppa ja palvelut** | Shopping, banking, libraries, complaints, customer service |
| 4 | **Kulttuuri** | Arts, hobbies, media, traditions, festivals |
| 5 | **Matkustaminen** | Travel, transportation, tourism, directions |
| 6 | **Terveys ja hyvinvointi** | Health, symptoms, doctor visits, wellbeing, emergencies |
| 7 | **Työ** | Work experience, job interviews, careers, professional life |
| 8 | **Ympäristö** | Environment, recycling, nature, climate, sustainability |
| 9 | **Yhteiskunta** | Society, taxes, voting, social issues, civic life |

Each task type (kertominen, keskustelu, tilanne, mielipide for speaking; informal, formal, mielipide for writing) can be generated for any of these topics at any level.

## 5. Key Guidance for Candidates (The "Hints")

- **No Scripts:** Candidates must speak spontaneously; they should not write full sentences during preparation [37-39].
- **Fillers:** Using filler words like _tota_, _niinku_, and _tuota_ is encouraged to keep the speech flowing [40-42].
- **Honesty:** Candidates do not need to tell the truth; they can invent details as long as the language is correct [17].
- **Politeness:** Encourage the use of conditional forms (e.g., _voisitko_) and polite particles (_-han/-hän_) in service situations [43, 44].
